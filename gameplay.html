Honkai: Star Rail is a turn-based RPG where players form a party of four characters and engage in strategic combat encounters. The game combines character collection, exploration, and a deep narrative with challenging endgame modes for long-term progression.

Combat System

Battles in Honkai: Star Rail unfold in turns determined by the Action Value (AV) system. Each character and enemy has a Speed stat; the lower their AV, the sooner they take their turn. This makes Speed a critical stat for controlling the pace of combat. There are no separate attack or defence phases — any character can act at any time based on AV order.

Skill Points (SP)
A shared team resource. Basic Attacks generate SP; Skills consume it. Managing SP carefully between attackers and supports is the core resource loop of every battle.
Ultimates & Energy
Characters build Energy from dealing and receiving damage. When their gauge is full, they can unleash a powerful Ultimate. Some characters use alternative mechanics — Acheron charges via debuffs, for example.
Weakness Break
Every enemy has a Toughness bar and one or more elemental weaknesses. Attacking with the correct element depletes Toughness; when it hits zero, the enemy is Broken — dealing a burst of damage, delaying their next turn, and triggering a Break Effect based on the element used.
Super Break
Introduced in Version 2.0, Super Break allows certain characters to deal massive bonus damage while an enemy is in a Broken state. It opened entirely new team-building archetypes centred around constant Breaks.
Follow-Up Attacks
Some characters can trigger additional attacks outside the normal turn order — either automatically or in response to teammate actions. These chain reactions can dramatically increase damage output.
DoT (Damage over Time)
Certain elements inflict status effects that deal damage each turn — Burn, Bleed, Shock, Wind Shear, and Erode. Stacking multiple DoTs on enemies is the core strategy of Nihility-focused teams.

Elements

Every character and enemy is associated with one of 7 elements. Attacking an enemy's weakness depletes their Toughness faster and triggers powerful Break Effects. Building a team that covers multiple weaknesses is essential for tackling tougher content.

Break Effects by Element

  • Fire — Inflicts Burn, dealing persistent fire damage each turn.
  • Ice — Freezes the enemy, preventing their action for one turn.
  • Lightning — Inflicts Shock, dealing persistent lightning damage each turn.
  • Wind — Inflicts Wind Shear, stacking damage-over-time up to 5 times.
  • Physical — Inflicts Bleed, dealing damage proportional to the enemy's max HP.
  • Quantum — Entangles the enemy, dealing damage and delaying their action.
  • Imaginary — Imprisons the enemy, reducing their Speed and delaying their next turn.

Paths & Combat Roles

Each character's Path defines their combat role. While Paths carry deep lore significance, in gameplay they serve as a flexible class system. The 8 playable Paths cover every role a team might need, and many characters can fill hybrid roles depending on how they're built.

  • Hunt & Destruction — Primary damage dealers. Hunt excels at single-target precision; Destruction mixes damage with self-sustain.
  • Erudition — AoE specialists, ideal for clearing multiple weaker enemies.
  • Nihility — Debuffers and DoT dealers. Best at sustained damage over many turns.
  • Harmony — Buffers who amplify the team's overall output.
  • Preservation — Shields and aggro management to protect allies.
  • Abundance — Healers who restore HP and provide sustain.
  • Remembrance — A newer hybrid Path focused on ice mechanics and summoned Memosprite companions.

Game Modes

Beyond the main story, Honkai: Star Rail offers a range of recurring game modes that refresh regularly and reward players who push their team-building skills. Most modes reset every few weeks and grant Stellar Jade — the premium currency used for Warps.

Simulated Universe Permanent
A roguelike mode where players run through procedurally structured worlds, collecting Blessings tied to the Aeons' Paths to power up their run. Supports various difficulty tiers including the advanced Divergent Universe variant.
Memory of Chaos Bi-weekly
The classic endgame mode. Two waves of increasingly difficult enemies must be cleared within a turn limit using two separate teams of four. The highest stages reward substantial Stellar Jade every two weeks.
Pure Fiction Bi-weekly
Introduced in Version 2.0. Unlike Memory of Chaos, Pure Fiction rewards hitting high damage numbers against large groups of enemies. AoE and DoT teams thrive here.
Apocalyptic Shadow Bi-weekly
Added in 2.0 alongside Pure Fiction. Focuses on boss fights where Break mechanics and specific elemental weaknesses play a central role. Break-focused teams perform extremely well.
Forgotten Hall Permanent
An older endgame mode with static, increasingly difficult enemy stages. Useful for new players ramping up their roster depth before tackling the rotating modes.
Story Missions Permanent
The core Trailblaze missions and Companion missions that advance the narrative. New story chapters are added with each major version update, approximately every 6 weeks.

Warp System (Gacha)

Characters and Light Cones (equipment) are obtained through Warps, Honkai: Star Rail's gacha system. There are several banner types, each using different currencies:

  • Limited Banners — Feature a specific 5★ character for a limited time. The 50/50 system means the first 5★ pull is a 50% chance of being the featured character; if it isn't, the next 5★ is guaranteed to be.
  • Standard Banner — A permanent pool of 5★ characters and Light Cones available indefinitely. Won pulls from 50/50 losses go here.
  • Light Cone Banners — Separate banners for a featured 5★ Light Cone (signature weapon), also using the 75/25 guarantee system.

The soft pity begins at pull 75, dramatically increasing 5★ rates. Hard pity guarantees a 5★ at pull 90. A 4★ is guaranteed within every 10 pulls. Pity carries over between banner periods.
