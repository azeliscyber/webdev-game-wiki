Honkai: Star Rail is set in a vast universe governed by mysterious, godlike beings called Aeons, each embodying a cosmic philosophical concept known as a Path. The central conflict revolves around Stellarons — catastrophic entities that threaten to destroy the worlds they infect. Players follow the Trailblazer, an amnesiac protagonist, aboard the legendary Astral Express.

Stellarons — The Cancer of All Worlds

Stellarons are mysterious, catastrophic entities widely described as the "Cancer of All Worlds." They are seeds of disaster that embed themselves within a world and gradually corrupt it from the inside out, triggering devastating phenomena called Stellaron Outbreaks that can destroy entire civilisations.

The origins of Stellarons remain unclear, but they are closely associated with the Stellaron Hunters — a rogue faction led by the prophet-like figure Elio, who collects and weaponises Stellarons in pursuit of a mysterious script intended to avert the Theory of Four Apocalypses.

The Trailblazer unwittingly becomes a Stellaron Host at the very start of the game, absorbing a Stellaron during the attack on Herta Space Station. Rather than becoming a test subject, they join the Astral Express crew and travel the stars to understand — and control — the entity within.

The Aeons — Gods of the Universe

Aeons are higher-dimensional, godlike beings of immense power who preside over the universe. Each Aeon is inextricably bound to a cosmic concept — their Path — which defines their nature, their actions, and the ideologies of their followers. Aeons are rarely seen directly, yet their influence shapes every corner of existence.

When a sentient being's will aligns completely with a cosmic concept and they ascend to Aeonhood, a Path is born or taken over. Aeons may also bestow fragments of their power upon chosen followers, transforming them into Emanators — among the most powerful individuals in the known universe.

There are 18 known Aeons in total, though not all are still active. Some have been destroyed, absorbed by rivals, or have voluntarily ceased activity. Their relationships with one another — alliances, enmities, and philosophical oppositions — form the hidden backbone of the game's lore.

Notable Aeons

  • Akivili (Trailblaze) — The Aeon who forged the Star Rail network, linking isolated worlds. The Astral Express travels in Akivili's name. Their fate is one of the story's lingering mysteries.
  • Nanook (Destruction) — The Aeon of annihilation, who destroyed their own world upon ascension. Nanook's followers, the Antimatter Legion, seek to bring destruction to all of existence.
  • Yaoshi (Abundance) — The Aeon of life and endless propagation. Revered as a healer by some, Yaoshi is hunted by the Xianzhou Alliance as a bringer of Mara — an affliction of immortality gone wrong.
  • Qlipoth (Preservation) — The ancient Aeon who built the Foundations of the Universe. The IPC follows the Path of Preservation, centred on stability and order.
  • IX (Nihility) — The Aeon of nothingness and meaninglessness. IX does not deliberately grant power, yet Emanators of Nihility exist because Nihility envelops all equally.

The Paths

Paths are congregations of imaginary energy, born as manifestations of universal philosophical concepts. A person whose will aligns with a Path can draw power from it, becoming a Pathstrider. In gameplay, Paths define a character's combat role and abilities. Currently, 8 Paths are playable out of 18 total.

Story Overview

The story begins on the Herta Space Station, a research facility orbiting an unknown planet, during a sudden attack by the Antimatter Legion. The Trailblazer, an amnesiac traveller, accidentally absorbs a Stellaron during the chaos and is taken aboard the Astral Express to avoid becoming a test subject.

The Express crew — including the enigmatic archivist Dan Heng, the enthusiastic photographer March 7th, and the seasoned navigator Himeko — journey together through several worlds:

  • Jarilo-VI — A frozen planet locked in endless winter, where the underground city of Belobog struggles beneath the weight of a Stellaron crisis and generational conflict.
  • Xianzhou Luofu — A planet-sized flagship of the Xianzhou Alliance, hunting Denizens of Abundance while wrestling with the curse of Mara — the price of immortality.
  • Penacony — The "Land of Dreams," a resort world built over an ancient prison. A dreamlike setting where family, grief, and the nature of reality become entwined in a conspiracy involving the Stellaron Hunters.
  • Amphoreus & Planarcadia — The most recent worlds, where ancient divine forces and the echoes of a forgotten civilisation challenge the Trailblazer in Version 4.3.

Key Factions

The Astral Express (The Nameless)

The protagonists of the story. The Astral Express crew follows the Path of Trailblaze — not a playable Path, but a lore concept tied to Akivili. They are known collectively as The Nameless, wanderers who travel the cosmos seeking adventure and resolving crises wherever they land.

The Stellaron Hunters

A band of extraordinarily powerful individuals united under the prophet Elio and his mysterious Script of Finality. Though they collect Stellarons and operate outside the law, their true goal is to prevent a catastrophe — the Theory of Four Apocalypses — from coming to pass. Members include Kafka, Silver Wolf, Blade, and Firefly.

The Interastral Peace Corporation (IPC)

The galaxy's dominant mercantile and peacekeeping body, following the Path of Preservation. The IPC controls trade routes, adjudicates disputes, and maintains order across worlds through economic dominance rather than military force.

The Xianzhou Alliance

A fleet of enormous ancient warships, each a world unto itself, bound together by a shared war against the Denizens of Abundance — creatures empowered by Yaoshi. The Alliance's crews are marked by the Luofu Curse, granting immortality at the cost of eventual Mara-induced madness.
